Tax Due Dates

The dates that matter most this year.

 ＋
  • Jan 31 — W-2s and 1099s should reach you
  • Apr 15 — Individual returns & Q1 estimated payments due
  • Jun 15 — Q2 estimated payments due
  • Sep 15 — Q3 estimated payments; extended partnership/S-corp returns due
  • Oct 15 — Extended individual returns due

Dates shift when they land on a weekend or holiday, and business deadlines vary by entity. We'll always confirm your specific dates — plan ahead, not after.

Tax Appointment Checklist

What to bring so we can get you done in one visit.

 ＋
  • Photo ID for you (and your spouse, if filing jointly)
  • Social Security cards for you and any dependents
  • Last year's tax return
  • W-2s, 1099s, and other income documents
  • Mortgage interest or property tax statements (1098)
  • Business or rental income and expense records
  • Health insurance forms (1095-A/B/C), if applicable
  • Childcare provider name, address, and tax ID
  • Any letters from the IRS or state this year
  • Voided check or account/routing numbers for direct deposit

Record Retention Guide

How long to actually keep your tax paperwork.

 ＋
  • Tax returns & supporting documents: at least 3 years, 7 years if you claimed a loss or bad debt
  • Property records (home, investments): keep as long as you own it, plus 3 years after you sell
  • Business records (payroll, receipts): 4–7 years
  • Copies of filed returns themselves: keep forever

When in doubt, keep it. Storage is cheap — audits aren't fun.
